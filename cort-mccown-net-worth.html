Cort McCown Full Overview and Wiki

The Journey of a Multifaceted Entertainer

Cort McCown, an American comedian and actor, has carved a unique niche in the entertainment world.

His journey began with an uncredited role in the 1985 hit film Teen Wolf, marking his entry into Hollywood. This debut, though small, set the stage for his future successes.

From Silver Screen to Stand-up

Following Teen Wolf, McCown landed a supporting role in the 1987 teen film Can’t Buy Me Love.

His filmography expanded with roles in Beverly Hills Brats and One Man Force. His versatility shone through his last film role in the 1992 comedy/horror Auntie Lee’s Meat Pies.

However, McCown’s career trajectory took a significant turn when he ventured into stand-up comedy in 2001, opening for legends like Andrew Dice Clay and Harland Williams.

A Regular in Comedy Circles

McCown is not just a comedian; he’s a staple in Los Angeles’ comedy scene. Regular performances at The World Famous Comedy Store and Laugh Factory have cemented his status as a comedy club favorite.

His transition from acting to comedy showcases his adaptability and talent in engaging diverse audiences.

The Playboy Comedy Tour: A Game Changer

In 2003, McCown’s career hit a new high with the creation of The Playboy Comedy Tour. Hosted at The Palms Hotel in Las Vegas, this tour not only won Best of Las Vegas several times but also marked McCown’s prowess as a comedy show host.

His role as the Morning Show host for Playboy Radio on Sirius Satellite Radio further diversified his portfolio.

International Fame and Podcast Ventures

McCown’s fame crossed borders with performances at the Edinburgh Fringe Festival and the Amsterdam International Comedy Festival.
